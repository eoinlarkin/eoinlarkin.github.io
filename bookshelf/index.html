Bookshelf

Business:

General business books covering everything from strategy to scandals:

  • The Innovator’s Dilemma (Clayton Christensen)
    Essential reading. Thought provoking book on why despite doing all the right things your business can still end up being overtaken by disrupters.
  • Janesville: An American Story (Amy Goldstein)
  • Barbarians At The Gate (Bryan Burrough & John Helyar)
    One of the best books on corporate finance and mergers and acquisitions. Still relevant today despite being more than 30 years old.
  • No Logo (Naomi Klein)
  • Steve Jobs (Walter Isaacson)
  • When Genius Failed: The Rise and Fall of LTCM
    Great book on the collapse of the original “quant” Hedge Fund
  • The Ride of a Lifetime (Robert Iger)
  • What It Takes: Lessons in the Pursuit of Excellence (Stephen A. Schwarzman)
  • Kochland: The Secret History of Koch Industries and Corporate Power in America (Christopher Leonard)
  • Range: How Generalists Triumph in a Specialized World (David Epstein)

Tech:

  • Bad Blood: Secrets and Lies in a Silicon Valley Startup (John Carreyrou)
  • The Hard Thing About Hard Things (Ben Horowitz)
  • In the Plex: How Google Thinks, Works, and Shapes Our Lives (Steven Levy)
  • No Filter: The Inside Story of Instagram (Sarah Frier)
  • Netflixed (Gina Keating)
  • That Will Never Work: The Birth of Netflix (Marc Randolph)
  • Banking on IT: How I Disrupted an Industry (Anne Boden)

2008 Financial Crisis:

  • Too Big to Fail (Andrew Ross Sorkin)
    The most comprehensive telling of the story of the Financial Crisis across the various institutations and personalities. If you are going to read one book, this is the one.
  • Flash Boys (Michael Lewis)
  • The Greatest Trade Ever (Gregory Zuckerman)
    Focuses on John Paulson’s bet against the US Housing Market and his investment in credit default swaps.
  • Fatal Risk: A Cautionary Tale of AIG’s Corporate Suicide (Roddy Boyd)
    A great book on AIG and some of the complex hedging that they were doing which eventually bankrupted the company.

2008 Financial Crisis - An Irish Perspective:

Books from the “Four Angry Men” that were published during the Financial Crisis. by Matt Cooper is the most authoritative in documenting the events, Fintan O’Toole’s book offers the best reflection on the wider societal issues

  • Ship of Fools: How Stupidity and Corruption Sank the Celtic Tiger (Fintan O’Toole)
  • Who Really Runs Ireland? (Matt Cooper)
  • Showtime: The Inside Story of Fianna Fail in Power (Pat Leahy)
  • The Bankers (Shane Ross)

Politics

  • Game Change (John Heilemann and Mark Halperin)
    Great book on the 2008 Presidential Election
  • Team of Rivals: The Political Genius of Abraham Lincoln (Doris Kearns Goodwin)

Misc.

  • The Lost City of Z (David Gann)
    A mad (in the best sense) recounting of the exploration of the Amazonian basin at the turn of the 20th Century.
  • What I Talk About When I Talk About Running (Haruki Murakami)
    A lovely book about running (among other topics).
  • Moonwalking with Einstein (Joshua Foer)
    Great book on memory training and how we can (and can’t) remember where we left our keys.
